Biography

Biography

Charles Thomas Munger, better known as Charlie Munger, is an American billionaire, investor, businessman and former lawyer in the real estate sector. He is the vice president of Berkshire Hathaway, the conglomerate controlled by Warren Buffett; Buffett has described Munger as his closest associate and right-hand man. Munger has served as a vice president of Wesco Financial Corporation from 1984 to 2011. He is also the president of the Daily Journal Corporation, a newspaper based in Los Angeles, California, and is the director of Costco Wholesale Corporation.


Charlie Munger was married to his first wife, Nancy Huggins, from 1945 to 1953. Their three children were Wendy, Molly, and Teddy. At the age of nine, Teddy died of leukemia. Munger exchanged wedding vows with Nancy Barry in 1956. They were married until her death on February 6, 2010. They had four children: Charles T. Munger Jr., Emilie Munger Ogden, Barry A. Munger, and Philip R. Munger. Munger also helped Barry raise her two sons from her previous marriage: William Harold Borthwick and David Borthwick.


Charlie Munger is a major donor to the University of Michigan, his former school. For more than 40 years, he served as a trustee of the Harvard-Westlake School. In October 2014, he revealed that he was making a $65 million donation to the Kavli Institute for Theoretical Physics at the University of California, Santa Barbara. In March 2016, he revealed that he was donating another $200 million to the institution.

Timeline

Birth

Charlie Munger was born January 1, 1924

Omaha, Nebraska. As a teenager, he worked at Buffett & Son, a grocery store owned by Warren Buffett's grandfather. His father, Alfred Case Munger, was a lawyer. His grandfather was Thomas Charles Munger, a district court judge and a state representative in the United States Capitol.

Study

University of Michigan 1941 - 1942

He studied mathematics at the University of Michigan between 1941 and 1942 and later served in the Army Air Corps as a meteorologist.

Lawyer

Harvard University 1948

He graduated cum laude in Law from Harvard University and moved with his family (wife and three children) to California, where he joined a law firm.

Muger, Tolles y Olson LLP

Founded and Work 1960

Charlie accepts Buffet's job offer (they had met in 1957 through a well-known doctor from Omaha) and finally leaves the practice of law at the firm he had founded in 1962 (Muger, Tolles and Olson LLP), to devote himself to investment Management.

Investment Firm

Wheeler, Munger & Co 1975

The investment firm created by Munger achieved a growth rate of close to 20% from 1962 to 1975, while the average annual rate of the Dow Jones for that period was 5%.

CEO and President

Wesco Financial Corporation 1984 - 2011

He served as CEO and Chairman of Wesco Financial Corporation, which is now a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway. Based in Pasadena, he was active in three main categories: insurance, furniture rental, and steel service.
